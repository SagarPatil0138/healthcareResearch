<h3 data-start="823" data-end="848"><strong data-start="827" data-end="848">Market Definition</strong></h3>
<p data-start="850" data-end="1449">Monoclonal antibodies (mAbs) are laboratory-produced molecules that can mimic the immune system&rsquo;s ability to fight off harmful pathogens such as viruses and bacteria. They are designed to target and bind to specific proteins or cells involved in various diseases. Monoclonal antibodies are used in the treatment of cancers, autoimmune diseases, infections, and other chronic conditions. The market for monoclonal antibodies is growing due to the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, advancements in biotechnology, and the growing adoption of targeted therapies for better therapeutic outcomes.</p>
