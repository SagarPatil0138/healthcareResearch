<h3 data-start="1176" data-end="1201"><strong data-start="1180" data-end="1201">Market Definition</strong></h3>
<p data-start="1203" data-end="1885">Aptamers are short, synthetic oligonucleotides (either DNA or RNA) that bind to specific target molecules such as proteins, small molecules, or cells. They exhibit high specificity and affinity, making them ideal for use in a variety of applications, including diagnostics, drug delivery, and as therapeutic agents. Aptamers can be generated through an in vitro process known as systematic evolution of ligands by exponential enrichment (SELEX), and they can perform similar roles to antibodies in terms of binding to their target molecule. Unlike antibodies, however, aptamers can be chemically synthesized, offering several advantages in terms of stability, cost, and versatility.</p>
